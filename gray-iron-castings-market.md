<p><p>The Gray Iron Castings market comprises two primary molding types: vertical molding and horizontal molding. Vertical molding involves pouring molten iron into a vertically positioned mold, allowing for efficient use of space and quick production cycles, making it suitable for large, complex parts. Horizontal molding, on the other hand, uses flat mold patterns laid on a horizontal plane, enabling the production of intricate designs and uniform thickness. Both methods are pivotal in various industries, including automotive and machinery, owing to their distinct advantages.</p></p>
