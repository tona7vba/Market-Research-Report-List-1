<p><p>The industrial wood adhesives market is primarily divided into two types: natural and synthetic adhesives. Natural adhesives, derived from renewable resources such as animal proteins, starches, and plant extracts, are eco-friendly and often used in applications requiring lower environmental impact. In contrast, synthetic adhesives, made from petrochemicals or polymers, offer superior performance characteristics, including stronger bonds and resistance to moisture and heat. Each type serves various applications in woodworking, furniture, and construction, catering to specific performance and sustainability needs.</p></p>
