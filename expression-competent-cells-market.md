<p><p>Expression competent cells are crucial in molecular biology for gene cloning and protein expression. The market is primarily divided into two types: chemically competent cells and electrocompetent cells. Chemically competent cells use heat shock or chemical treatment to facilitate DNA uptake, offering a cost-effective option for many applications. In contrast, electrocompetent cells utilize an electric field to introduce DNA, enabling higher transformation efficiencies, particularly for larger plasmids. Each type caters to different experimental needs and performance criteria in genetic research.</p></p>
