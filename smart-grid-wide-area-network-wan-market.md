<p><p>The Smart Grid Wide Area Network (WAN) market includes both wired and wireless segments. Wired WAN utilizes robust infrastructure, such as fiber optics and coaxial cables, to facilitate reliable, high-bandwidth communication for energy management and monitoring. In contrast, wireless WAN employs technologies like cellular networks, satellite, and mesh networks, offering flexibility and easier deployment in remote areas. Both types play a crucial role in connecting smart devices within the grid, enhancing operational efficiency, and improving energy distribution.</p></p>
