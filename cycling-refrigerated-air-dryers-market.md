<p><p>Cycling refrigerated air dryers utilize a refrigeration cycle to remove moisture from compressed air, categorized primarily into air-cooled and water-cooled types. Air-cooled dryers use ambient air for cooling and are ideal for smaller systems or locations with limited water resources. They are typically more cost-effective and easier to install. Conversely, water-cooled dryers utilize water for cooling, making them suitable for larger systems requiring higher efficiency and consistent performance in industrial settings, particularly in environments with higher ambient temperatures.</p></p>
