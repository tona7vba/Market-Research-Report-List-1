<p><p>The textile dyes market comprises several types, each suited for different fabrics and needs. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetics like polyester, providing vibrant shades. Reactive dyes bond with fibers, ideal for cellulose fibers, offering excellent wash fastness. Sulfur dyes are used for cotton and other materials, known for their cost-effectiveness and deep hues. Vat dyes are used for cellulose fibers and offer excellent light and wash fastness. Acid dyes are suited for nylon and wool, while other specialty dyes cater to unique applications.</p></p>
