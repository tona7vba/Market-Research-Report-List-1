<p><p>The implantable presbyopia correction devices market includes corneal inlays and scleral implants. Corneal inlays are small devices inserted into the cornea to enhance depth of field and improve near vision, suitable for individuals with presbyopia. Scleral implants, on the other hand, are placed on the sclera and create a dynamic optical surface, allowing for better focusing capabilities. Both options offer innovative solutions for age-related vision decline, catering to diverse patient needs and preferences in vision correction.</p></p>
