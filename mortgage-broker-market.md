<p><ul><li>Charge</li><li>Free</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The mortgage broker market can be classified into two primary types: charge and free markets. In a charge market, brokers earn fees directly from clients, typically for their services and expertise in finding suitable mortgage options. Conversely, in a free market, brokers are compensated through commissions paid by lenders when a loan is successfully arranged, creating little to no cost for borrowers. Both systems aim to facilitate access to mortgage options, but they differ in cost structure and client relationships.</p></p>
