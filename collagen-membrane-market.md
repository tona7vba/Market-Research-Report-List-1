<p><p>The collagen membrane market is primarily segmented into two types: cowhide and pigskin. Cowhide collagen membranes are derived from bovine sources and are known for their strength and biocompatibility, making them suitable for dental and orthopedic surgeries. In contrast, pigskin collagen membranes come from porcine sources and are valued for their excellent permeability and ease of use in various tissue engineering applications. Both types are utilized in regenerative medicine, with preferences depending on specific clinical requirements and regulatory considerations.</p></p>
