Analog printing involves traditional techniques, such as screen printing or rotary printing, which allow for high-volume production and cost-effectiveness, making it suitable for large-scale applications. In contrast, digital printing utilizes inkjet or laser technology, offering greater flexibility, customization, and precision, which is ideal for intricate designs and smaller runs. Both types cater to diverse industries, including electronics, automotive, and decorative ceramics.</p></p>
