<p><p>The Non-Dispersive Infrared (NDIR) gas sensor market is categorized into three types based on infrared wavelength ranges: near-infrared, mid-infrared, and far-infrared. Near-infrared sensors, operating between 0.75 to 1.5 micrometers, are commonly used for measuring gases like CO2 in various applications. Mid-infrared sensors, covering 2.5 to 25 micrometers, are ideal for detecting gases like methane and hydrocarbons. Far-infrared sensors, operating above 25 micrometers, primarily focus on industrial and environmental monitoring, offering advantages in specificity and sensitivity.</p></p>
