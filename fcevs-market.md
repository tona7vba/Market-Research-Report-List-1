<p><p>FCEVs, or Fuel Cell Electric Vehicles, are vehicles powered by hydrogen fuel cells that generate electricity through a chemical reaction between hydrogen and oxygen, emitting only water vapor as a byproduct. The FCEVs market is expected to grow at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 13.1% during the forecast period. This growth is fueled by rising demand for zero-emission vehicles, particularly in regions like Europe, North America, and Asia-Pacific, where governments are implementing stringent emission regulations and offering subsidies for hydrogen-powered vehicles.</p><p>Key trends in the FCEVs market include the development of hydrogen refueling infrastructure, partnerships between automakers and energy companies, and the introduction of new FCEV models across various vehicle segments, including passenger cars, buses, and trucks. Additionally, advancements in fuel cell technology, such as improved efficiency and reduced costs, are making FCEVs more accessible to consumers. The market is also witnessing increased investments in green hydrogen production, which is expected to further boost the adoption of FCEVs in the coming years.</p></p>
